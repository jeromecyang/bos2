Visiting Glacier, Jasper and Banff National Parks from Calgary
June 30 - July 4, 2017
Why did I go?
After Yellowstone and Grand Teton, I had been thinking of visiting Glacier National Park, the northernmost US national park in the Rockies, but its remoteness had been an issue. Until this year, I realized the closest big city to Glacier National Park is: Calgary, Alberta, Canada! Therefore I buffered my July 4th weekend, used my friends' place as a basecamp, and visited Glacier, Jasper and Banff - three national parks over a long weekend! What I did there are mostly: hiking and camping alone, enjoying the scenic drive, visiting destinations not in my plan, and drinking straight from glacier water! Coming from a subtropical country, I rarely had a chance to spot glacier landscapes, let alone active glaciers. During this trip, however, I was always surrounded by glaciers, snow-capped mountains, temperate forests, U-shaped valleys, and occasionally, wildlife.
